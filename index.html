Why now

The most powerful analytical tools ever built are waiting for your data.

AI systems can now reason across your health data with a depth and speed that simply did not exist before. But they are only as good as what you feed them. Raw numbers produce generic answers. Your data with your context attached produces something entirely different.

Me. captures that context through daily check-ins, voice notes, and annotations that take under 90 seconds. How you slept. How you felt. What changed. What you noticed. That layer is what transforms a spreadsheet of numbers into a story an AI, a physician, or a coach can actually reason from.

The same output that an AI reasons across, your physician can read before your appointment. Your coach can act on before your next session. Your therapist can reference without you having to reconstruct everything from memory. The data is the same. The audience is whoever you choose.

Me. doesn't decide who analyses your data.

It produces output that is formatted for AI, legible for clinicians, and actionable for coaches. The direction is yours. The quality is consistent.

ChatGPT. Claude. Gemini. Your GP. Your coach. Your therapist. All of them. None of them. Your call.

What becomes possible

The use cases are
as unlimited as the data.

Me. doesn't decide what you do with your health data. It just makes sure you can do anything with it.

The athlete

You've been tracking every workout, every recovery score, every night of sleep. Me. compiles it alongside your annotations — the note that said legs felt dead by kilometre three, the check-in that flagged a poor night before the long run — into something your coach can actually reason from.

The patient

You walk into your GP appointment with 90 days of annotated health data. Sleep, HRV, energy, mood, the week you changed medication, the fortnight you felt off. Not screenshots. A coherent, structured story.

The curious

What's your biological age? What's actually driving your energy? Me. compiles your data — numbers and context together — into a structured output ready to drop into ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or any AI platform so you can ask the questions no health app will answer for you.

The investigator

Something has felt off for weeks. You can't explain it. The data alone won't tell you why. But the data plus your daily annotations — energy, mood, stress, sleep quality, what changed — builds a picture that actually has answers in it.

The optimiser

Is the new training block working? Did the supplement make a difference? Your biometrics tell part of the story. Your annotations tell the rest. Compile both. Compare. Find out.

The longevity seeker

You're not sick. You're paying attention. You want a long-term record that compounds over time: biometrics, annotations, context, all of it structured and portable so the picture only gets clearer as the years go on.

These are six. There are thousands more. Me. doesn't need to know yours.
